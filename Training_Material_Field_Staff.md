# Purpose of This Guide

This training guide helps field staff **collect reliable agricultural survey data** used in crop insurance analytics.

Accurate field data ensures that:

- insurance premiums are priced fairly
- claims are processed faster
- farmers receive support during drought or crop failure
- agricultural risk models remain reliable

Every survey you complete contributes directly to **protecting smallholder farmers.**

---

# Why Data Quality Matters

Recent analysis of **10,000 agricultural records** showed that crop yield is often **very difficult to predict** using common variables such as:

- rainfall
- soil nutrients (NPK)
- temperature

This is normal for smallholder agriculture, where many hidden factors affect production.

However, **poor or incomplete survey data makes this problem worse** and can lead to:

- incorrect insurance risk models
- delayed claim payments
- reduced trust from farmers

### Our Solution

Two practices significantly improve data reliability:

1. **Honest reporting of all field observations**
2. **Capturing one clear field photo for every survey**

These allow analysts to verify information using **satellite data and remote monitoring tools**.

---

# Mandatory Survey Checklist

Every survey must follow these steps.

### 1. Complete All Fields

- Do not leave any fields blank.
- If a farmer does not know the answer, record **“Unknown”** instead.

---

### 2. Verify Date Accuracy

Ensure:

- **Planting Date occurs before Harvest Date**

Incorrect dates cause major errors in agricultural models.

---

### 3. Record Yield Honestly

Even if crop yield is **very low**, report the true value.

Low yield data helps identify:

- drought events
- pest outbreaks
- insurance claim triggers

---

### 4. Capture One Field Photo

A **clear photo of the crop or soil** must be taken during every survey.

Photos help analysts:

- verify crop condition
- compare field data with satellite imagery
- detect abnormal reports

---

### 5. Flag Suspicious Records

If any unusual conditions occur, flag them in the survey form.

Examples:

| Situation | Action |
|------|------|
| Yield below 2 t/ha | Mark **High Risk – Photo Required** |
| Rainfall inconsistent with season | Add note in comments |
| Severe pest damage | Record outbreak and attach photo |

---

### 6. Submit Surveys Immediately

- Upload the completed survey as soon as possible.
- Avoid editing records later unless instructed by the support team.

Timely submission helps analysts monitor risks **in near real-time.**

# New Questions Added to Survey Forms

The following questions have been added to improve risk prediction.

Field staff must now ask farmers:

### Irrigation Effectiveness
Options:

- Good
- Average
- Poor

---

### Pest Outbreak

Has there been a pest outbreak recently?

Options:

- Yes (photo required)
- No

---

### Distance to Nearest Market

Record the estimated **distance in kilometers** to the closest market where crops are sold.

Market access often affects farming outcomes.

# What Happens After Submission

Once your survey is uploaded:

1. Data passes through **automated quality checks.**
2. Suspicious records are flagged by the analytics system.
3. The support desk may contact you **within 24 hours** if clarification is needed.

This system helps ensure:

- accurate agricultural data
- fair insurance decisions
- faster claim processing
